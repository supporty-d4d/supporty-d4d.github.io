CONTESTED LOGISTICS-AS-A-SERVICE · D4D SEOUL 2026

We give back the 6 minutes
that cost lives.

SUPPORTY is a contested logistics command system that enables a single operator to simultaneously control ground and aerial unmanned assets over a hybrid 4G/5G ↔ Starlink link. When resupply is delayed, what you lose isn't time — it's people.

00 · ORIGIN — Based on a true account

It started with
a single Telegram call.

We build hybrid communication modules that seamlessly switch between 4G/5G cellular and Starlink satellite links. Our edge: platform-agnostic control with no distance limitations, for any asset in the air or on the ground. We have exported units to Jordan and the UAE, and are preparing operational validation in Ukraine.

This project was born from direct interviews with Ukrainian soldiers and volunteers. Our answer to the single-operator multi-UxV challenge is to redesign the PMC model — shifting its center of gravity from combat operations to contested logistics support.

01 · PROBLEM

One asset. One pilot.
This model costs lives.

Current unmanned systems operations scale linearly: more assets require proportionally more personnel. In South Korea and across the APAC region, where force sizes are shrinking, this model is unsustainable — and the cost is paid at the most critical moment on the battlefield.

"Six minutes sooner, and I would have kept my leg."
— Ukrainian frontline interview · Foot team ETA: 40 min. Vehicle entry: impossible due to exposure risk.
